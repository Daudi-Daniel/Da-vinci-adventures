Food Adventure — Feature Notes

Mobile app where users upload meal photos → AI asks clarifying questions → app estimates calories and logs intake.
Tracks daily/weekly/monthly totals, shows trends, offers personalized guidance, recipes, nearby food joints, social features and event sharing, plus activity suggestions and calorie-burn estimates.
Core feature set



    Photo-based meal analysis


Image recognition + segmentation to identify items on plate.
Follow-up Q&A (portion size, ingredients, cooking method) to refine estimates.
Portion-size estimation aids: reference object, user-entered portion, or scale-based input.


    Calorie & nutrient calculation


Map recognized items to nutrition database (USDA/FoodData Central or commercial API).
Return calories + basic macro split (protein, fat, carbs) and optionally micronutrients.


    Logging & aggregation


Per-meal logs with timestamp, tags, photos.
Aggregates: daily, weekly, monthly totals and averages.
Visual trends and comparisons to goals.


    Personalized recommendations


Weekly summaries with benefits/risks, progress notes, and suggestions for next week.
Tailored tips: portion control, ingredient swaps, meal timing.
Premium features
Deeper food benefits/risks analysis and personalized nutrition coaching content.
Exercise planning: suggested workouts and estimated calories burned.
Integrated intake/output tracking and progress reports, exportable data.


    Recipes & discovery


Recipe library, user-submitted recipes, ingredient lists, calorie per serving.
Save/clone recipes and auto-add to log.


    Maps & local discovery


Show nearby food joints, user ratings, approximate menu calorie highlights.
Directions and event locations.


    Social & community


Feed for recipes, photos, kitchen shots, events, cookouts, cookathons.
Event creation and RSVP, group challenges (marathon, hikes).
Share routes, mileage, and estimated calories burned for group activities.


    Activity routing & calorie burn


Suggest nearby walking/hiking routes with distance, elevation, and estimated calories burned (based on user weight/pace).
Integration options: HealthKit/Google Fit, Strava, GPS.
AI & technical considerations



    Models & pipelines


Image model for food recognition (fine-tuned vision model).
Multimodal LLM for follow-up Q&A, portion inference, and explanation text.
Nutrition lookup service that maps labels to database entries.



    User interaction


Show confidence score; ask clarifying questions when confidence is low.
Allow user corrections and reuse corrections to improve personalization.
Portion estimation techniques
Depth/size via dual-camera or reference object, user-entered measures, or slider-based portion scaling.


    Data sources


Food databases: USDA FoodData Central, Open Food Facts, commercial nutrition APIs for completeness.
Exercise MET values or fitness APIs for calorie burn estimates.
