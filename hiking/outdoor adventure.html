Outdoor Adventure — Feature Notes

Routes, operators, bookings, safety and social — compact spec (styled) 

This app surfaces adventure routes and upcoming guided trips → users can browse routes, inspect operator details, pricing and logistics, then book or save trips for planning.

Core feature set


Route discovery & preview

Search nearby or anywhere by filters: difficulty, distance, elevation gain, scenery, duration, accessibility.
Route preview: map polyline, elevation profile, photos, POIs, estimated time & calories burned.
Difficulty badge, best season, permits required, surface and gear notes.


Guided trips & operators

Operator profiles with ratings, schedules, pricing, transport options, itinerary, inclusions/exclusions, insurance & cancellation policies.
Upcoming trip calendar and booking flow; group size, skill requirements and refunds.


Booking & planning

Save routes/trips to itinerary; sync with calendar; flexible dates and payment (Stripe).
Packing/gear checklists and pre-trip briefings; suggested accommodations and transport.


Maps & route guidance

Interactive map (Google/Mapbox) with offline route downloads, turn-by-turn guidance, GPX/KML export and shareable route links.
Integration with GPS devices, Strava, Garmin.


Safety & logistics

Emergency contacts, nearest hospitals, live weather, avalanche/fire alerts, trail closures and permit guidance.
Route risk score and required skill badges; suggested contingency plans.


Social & community

Feed for trip photos, route reviews, meetup events, group challenges and story posts.
Event creation, RSVP, group chat, shared GPX routes and trip reports.


Activity metrics & calorie burn

Estimate calories burned using distance, elevation and user weight/pace (METs).
Track time, distance, elevation gain, pace; compare weekly/monthly totals and set goals.


AI & technical considerations

Personalized recommendations based on user history, fitness level and available free time.
Route-summarization, risk inference, equipment Q&A, and operator-matching using ML and an LLM assistant.
Confidence scores for AI suggestions; allow user corrections and feedback loop.


Data sources & integrations

Trail data: OpenStreetMap, Hiking Project, AllTrails-style APIs, and local park services.
Weather: OpenWeatherMap, Meteo APIs. Events & operators: ingest ICS/JSON feeds or partner APIs.
Fitness: Google Fit, Apple HealthKit, Strava. Payments: Stripe.


Privacy, offline & scaling

Offline maps & routes stored locally with opt-in sync. Encrypted personal data, GDPR/CCPA awareness.
Backend: tile caching, routing services, job queue for data imports, admin tools for operator onboarding.

MVP recommendation
- Route discovery + map preview + basic route metrics.
- Operator trip listing + simple booking flow.
- Save itinerary + calendar sync + shareable GPX.
- Basic social feed + event RSVP.

Metrics to track
- Routes discovered, bookings, DAU/WAU, average trip group size, user-reported route accuracy.
- Safety alerts acknowledged and community engagement.

Next steps

- Choose maps/routing providers and payment provider.
- Prototype map UI, route preview and operator card.
